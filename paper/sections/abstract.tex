\begin{abstract}
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Vision Transformers offer a promising path to
conditional computation, yet pruning individual neurons within expert
sub-networks remains unstable: standard Lagrangian FLOPs penalties
produce per-neuron gradients that are orders of magnitude weaker than
task gradients (${\sim}10^{-4}$ vs.\ ${\sim}10^{-2}$), causing
\emph{uniform mask collapse} rather than selective pruning. We present
\textbf{KaleidoNet}, an elastic MoE Vision Transformer that replaces
Lagrangian budget control with a \emph{cubic sparsity schedule} paired
with gradient masking and a dual-rate optimizer. A deterministic
schedule ramps target sparsity from 0\% to 70\% following a cubic
curve, while hard gradient masking freezes pruned neurons permanently
and a $3{\times}$ higher learning rate on mask logits accelerates
structural adaptation. Through systematic experiments on four image
classification benchmarks (CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, Tiny-ImageNet,
STL-10), we demonstrate that KaleidoNet achieves a consistent
$\mathbf{1.8{\times}}$ FLOPs reduction while retaining
$\mathbf{85\text{--}93\%}$ of dense-model accuracy. Ablations over
eight architectural configurations reveal that elastic pruning and MoE
routing provide complementary benefits, with the cubic schedule being
the critical enabler of stable convergence. Model surgery converts the
soft masks into physically compact networks, yielding a
$\mathbf{1.63{\times}}$ parameter compression with no additional
accuracy loss.
\end{abstract}
