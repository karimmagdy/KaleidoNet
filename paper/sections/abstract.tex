\begin{abstract}
Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) Vision Transformers offer a promising path to
conditional computation, yet pruning individual neurons within expert
sub-networks remains unstable: standard Lagrangian FLOPs penalties
produce per-neuron gradients that are orders of magnitude weaker than
task gradients (${\sim}10^{-4}$ vs.\ ${\sim}10^{-2}$), causing
\emph{uniform mask collapse} rather than selective pruning. We propose
and analyze \textbf{KaleidoNet}, an elastic MoE Vision Transformer
that replaces Lagrangian budget control with a \emph{cubic sparsity
schedule} paired with gradient masking and a dual-rate optimizer. A
deterministic schedule ramps target sparsity from 0\% to 70\%
following a cubic curve, while hard gradient masking freezes pruned
neurons permanently and a $3{\times}$ higher learning rate on mask
logits accelerates structural adaptation. In preliminary experiments
with a limited training budget (5{,}000 steps) on CIFAR-10/100,
Tiny-ImageNet, and STL-10, KaleidoNet achieves a consistent
$\mathbf{1.8{\times}}$ FLOPs reduction while retaining
$\mathbf{85\text{--}87\%}$ of dense-model accuracy; however, under
this short schedule, KaleidoNet does not yet outperform simpler
baselines such as magnitude or random structured pruning, indicating
that longer training is needed for the cubic schedule's advantages to
materialize. Full convergence experiments are ongoing. Ablations over
eight architectural configurations reveal that elastic pruning and MoE
routing provide complementary benefits, and model surgery converts the
soft masks into physically compact networks, yielding a
$\mathbf{1.63{\times}}$ parameter compression with no additional
accuracy loss.
\end{abstract}
